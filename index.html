Weekly Rhythm
What Macy does every week during camp season (Jun 9 – Aug 29). Same cadence every week — gets easier as summer goes.

The Engine

Once the automated emails are set up and ads are running, the weekly rhythm is what keeps everything moving. Most weeks this is 3–4 hours of focused work. The rest runs itself.

DayTaskTime needed
Monday Check enrollment numbers for every program across all 3 locations. Flag anything under 80% — those go in Friday's email. Flag anything under 70% — prep urgency blast for Wednesday. 20 min
Tuesday If any program is under 70% for next week → write urgency blast email (15 min, no design) → send to Tommy for approval. 15–30 min (if needed)
Wednesday Send urgency blast if triggered + approved. Otherwise: capture content from LDs (photos, clips) for Friday email and social. 15–30 min
Thursday Draft Friday email — plain text, under 150 words, 1 photo, named open spots. Submit to Tommy for approval by end of day. Also post Kid of the Week on Instagram. 30–45 min
Friday 8am Tommy approves → Macy sends email. Post Instagram daily story recap from that week. Relist Facebook Marketplace every 10 days (30 seconds). 15 min
All week Monitor Facebook community groups (South Bay Moms, MB Parents, etc.) — respond immediately to any "summer camp recommendations?" posts. Post on NextDoor 1–2x per week in neighbor tone. 30 min/day

What Runs Automatically (Zero Macy Time)

Active Campaign fires Email 1 (on registration), Email 2 (21 days pre-camp), Email 3 (5 days pre-camp), Email 4 (2 wks pre-camp), and Email 5 (post-week upsell) automatically for every family all summer. Google Ads and Meta ads run continuously within budget. Once set up correctly in April, none of these need weekly attention.
